📱 How to Get Accurate Vital Signs

Visual Step-by-Step Guide

⚠️ Critical Requirement:

Camera-based vital signs measurement uses PPG (Photoplethysmography) technology, which requires BRIGHT LIGHTING to detect tiny color changes in your fingertip as blood pulses through. Without proper lighting and technique, you'll get the same demo values (150 BPM) instead of real measurements!

✅ Correct vs ❌ Wrong Finger Placement

✅ CORRECT
📷
👆
  • Finger completely covers camera
  • Flat against lens (no gaps)
  • Gentle pressure - feel glass
  • Index finger (best blood flow)
  • Stay completely still
  • Full 30 seconds
❌ WRONG
📷
👆
  • Finger angled (gaps let light in)
  • Not covering completely
  • Pressing too hard (blocks blood)
  • Using thumb (thicker skin)
  • Moving during measurement
  • Stopping too early

📋 Step-by-Step Instructions

1️⃣
💡 Bright Lighting

Turn on ALL lights in the room. The brighter, the better! Use overhead lights + desk lamp. PPG requires bright light to work.

2️⃣
🧽 Clean Camera

Wipe your camera lens with a soft cloth. Fingerprints, dust, or smudges will reduce signal quality significantly.

3️⃣
🧘 Relax First

Sit comfortably for 2-3 minutes. Don't measure immediately after exercise, caffeine, or stress. Let your heart rate settle.

4️⃣
📱 Position Phone

Place phone flat on a stable surface (table, desk). Holding phone in hand causes too much movement for accurate measurement.

5️⃣
👆 Place Finger

Use your INDEX finger. Gently place fingertip on camera lens, covering it completely with no gaps. Press just enough to feel the glass.

6️⃣
▶ Start Measurement

Click "Start Measurement" button. Keep your finger steady - DO NOT MOVE for the entire 30 seconds. Breathe normally.

7️⃣
👀 Watch Feedback

Watch the real-time feedback text. You MUST see "✅ Great signal!" or "📊 Good signal" for accurate results. If you see "⚠️ Weak" or "❌ Very weak", STOP and fix your setup!

8️⃣
⏱️ Wait 30 Seconds

Let the full 30 seconds complete. The progress bar will show you how much time is left. Don't press Stop button early!

✅ Do's and ❌ Don'ts

✅ DO

  • ✅ Use BRIGHT room lighting
  • ✅ Turn on ALL lights (overhead + desk lamp)
  • ✅ Use index finger for best results
  • ✅ Cover camera completely (no gaps)
  • ✅ Press gently - just feel the glass
  • ✅ Stay completely still for 30 seconds
  • ✅ Place phone on stable surface
  • ✅ Clean camera lens before measuring
  • ✅ Relax for 2-3 minutes first
  • ✅ Breathe normally - don't hold breath
  • ✅ Trust results with >60% quality
  • ✅ Measure at same time daily for trends

❌ DON'T

  • ❌ Use in dark room (PPG won't work!)
  • ❌ Use dim lighting (causes low quality)
  • ❌ Use thumb (thicker skin, less accurate)
  • ❌ Leave gaps around camera lens
  • ❌ Press too hard (blocks blood flow)
  • ❌ Move during measurement
  • ❌ Hold phone in hand (too unstable)
  • ❌ Measure with dirty camera lens
  • ❌ Measure right after exercise/caffeine
  • ❌ Talk or hold breath during measurement
  • ❌ Trust results with <40% quality
  • ❌ Stop measurement early (need full 30s)

🔧 Quick Troubleshooting

❌ Getting Same Values (150/95/55/12)

Problem: Signal quality too low → system can't detect real signal

Fix: Turn on MORE lights! Brighten the room dramatically. Cover camera fully. Press gently, not hard.

❌ "Very Low Signal Quality" Alert

Problem: Camera can't detect PPG signal

Fix: 1) Brighten room (critical!), 2) Clean camera lens, 3) Check finger covers camera completely, 4) Press lighter

❌ Flat Waveform

Problem: No pulsatile signal detected

Fix: Use index finger (better blood flow), warm up cold hands, use MUCH brighter lighting, stay completely still

❌ Values Jump Around

Problem: Motion artifacts, noise in signal

Fix: Stay COMPLETELY still, place phone on table (not handheld), don't talk, measure full 30 seconds

💡 Pro Tips for Best Results

🌟 Maximize Accuracy:
  • Lighting is EVERYTHING! PPG technology REQUIRES bright light. Turn on every light you can!
  • Consistency matters: Measure at the same time daily (morning after waking = most consistent)
  • Warm hands: Cold fingers have poor circulation → weak signal. Rub hands together first.
  • Patience: Don't rush! Full 30 seconds is needed for accurate measurements.
  • Track trends: One measurement = snapshot. Track over days/weeks for meaningful insights.
  • Environment: Quiet, relaxed setting. No stress, no movement, no distractions.
  • Only trust >60% quality: If signal quality is below 60%, the results are not reliable!
