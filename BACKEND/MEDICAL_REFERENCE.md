# 💊 AI DOCTOR - MEDICAL REFERENCE GUIDE

## ACCURATE MEDICINES BY CONDITION

This guide ensures the AI Doctor provides SPECIFIC, ACCURATE medications based on the exact viral or bacterial infection diagnosed.

## 🦠 VIRAL INFECTIONS

### 1. **Influenza (Flu)**
**Diagnosis Criteria:**
- Sudden onset high fever (>101°F)
- Body aches, fatigue
- Cough, sore throat
- Headache

**Treatment:**
```
MEDICINES:
- Oseltamivir (Tamiflu) 75mg - Take 1 capsule twice daily for 5 days
  (Most effective if started within 48 hours of symptom onset)
- Paracetamol (Tylenol) 500mg - Every 6 hours for fever/pain
- Cetirizine (Zyrtec) 10mg - Once daily for runny nose
- Throat lozenges - As needed

VACCINES:
- Annual Influenza Vaccine (Quadrivalent)
  Schedule: Every year before flu season (September-November)
- COVID-19 Booster (if due)

FOLLOW-UP:
- If fever >103°F or breathing difficulty → Emergency
- If no improvement in 3-4 days → Return for evaluation
```

---

### 2. **COVID-19**
**Diagnosis Criteria:**
- Fever, dry cough
- Loss of taste/smell
- Fatigue, body aches
- Difficulty breathing (severe cases)

**Treatment:**
```
MEDICINES:
MILD CASES:
- Paracetamol 500mg - Every 6 hours for fever
- Vitamin C 1000mg - Twice daily
- Vitamin D3 2000IU - Once daily
- Zinc 50mg - Once daily
- Plenty of fluids and rest

MODERATE CASES:
- Azithromycin 500mg - Day 1, then 250mg for 4 days (if bacterial co-infection)
- Dexamethasone 6mg - Once daily (if oxygen saturation <94%)
- Anticoagulants (if hospitalized)

VACCINES:
- COVID-19 Vaccine Primary Series (2 doses)
- COVID-19 Booster Doses (6 months after last dose)

FOLLOW-UP:
- Monitor oxygen saturation (SpO2) - if <94% → Hospital
- Fever >5 days → Medical evaluation
- Breathing difficulty → Emergency
```

---

### 3. **Viral Fever (Non-specific)**
**Diagnosis Criteria:**
- Fever without clear cause
- Mild body aches
- Fatigue
- No respiratory symptoms

**Treatment:**
```
MEDICINES:
- Paracetamol 650mg - Every 6-8 hours (max 4g/day)
- Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS) - Plenty of fluids
- Multivitamin - Once daily

VACCINES:
- Influenza Vaccine (if not recently taken)
- Ensure routine vaccines are up-to-date

FOLLOW-UP:
- If fever persists >3 days → Blood tests (CBC, malaria, dengue)
- Watch for rash, bleeding → Dengue suspicion
```

---

### 4. **Dengue Fever** ⚠️ CRITICAL
**Diagnosis Criteria:**
- High fever with severe headache (behind eyes)
- Joint/muscle pain ("breakbone fever")
- Rash (appears 3-4 days after fever)
- Nausea, vomiting
- Low platelet count

**Treatment:**
```
MEDICINES:
⚠️ PARACETAMOL ONLY - NO ASPIRIN/IBUPROFEN (bleeding risk!)
- Paracetamol 500mg - Every 6 hours ONLY
- IV Fluids (if hospitalized)
- Platelet monitoring essential

VACCINES:
- Dengvaxia (for those with prior dengue infection)

URGENT ACTION:
- Platelet count <100,000 → Hospital admission
- Severe abdominal pain, bleeding, restlessness → Emergency
- Monitor platelets daily

FOLLOW-UP:
- Complete Blood Count (CBC) - Daily monitoring
- Hospitalize if Warning Signs appear
```

### 5. **Chickenpox (Varicella)**
**Diagnosis Criteria:**
- Itchy rash with fluid-filled blisters
- Fever
- Fatigue
- Typically in children

**Treatment:**
```
MEDICINES:
- Acyclovir (Zovirax) 800mg - 5 times daily for 7 days
  (Start within 24 hours of rash onset)
- Calamine Lotion - Apply 3-4 times daily for itching
- Paracetamol 500mg - For fever (NO ASPIRIN in children - Reye's syndrome risk)
- Antihistamine (Hydroxyzine) 25mg - At bedtime if severe itching

VACCINES:
- Varicella Vaccine (2 doses)
  Dose 1: 12-15 months
  Dose 2: 4-6 years
- Post-exposure prophylaxis (within 3-5 days of exposure)

FOLLOW-UP:
- Secondary bacterial infection (pus, warmth) → Antibiotics needed
- High fever >3 days → Evaluation
```

---

### 6. **Herpes Simplex (Cold Sores / Genital Herpes)**
**Diagnosis Criteria:**
- Painful blisters on lips/genitals
- Tingling before outbreak
- Fever, swollen lymph nodes

**Treatment:**
```
MEDICINES:
ORAL:
- Acyclovir (Zovirax) 400mg - 5 times daily for 5 days
  OR
- Valacyclovir (Valtrex) 500mg - Twice daily for 5 days
  OR
- Famciclovir (Famvir) 250mg - Three times daily for 5 days

TOPICAL:
- Acyclovir cream 5% - Apply 5 times daily

FOR SUPPRESSION (Recurrent cases):
- Valacyclovir 500mg - Once daily continuously

VACCINES:
- No vaccine currently available

FOLLOW-UP:
- Start at first sign of outbreak
- Avoid triggers (stress, sun exposure)
```

---

### 7. **Viral Gastroenteritis (Stomach Flu)**
**Diagnosis Criteria:**
- Diarrhea (watery)
- Vomiting
- Stomach cramps
- Mild fever

**Treatment:**
```
MEDICINES:
- Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS) - Drink frequently
- Ondansetron (Zofran) 4mg - Every 8 hours for vomiting
- Probiotics (Saccharomyces boulardii) - Twice daily
- Zinc Sulfate 20mg - Once daily for 10-14 days

AVOID:
- Anti-diarrheal medications initially (let virus clear)

VACCINES:
- Rotavirus Vaccine (for infants)
  2-3 doses starting at 2 months

FOLLOW-UP:
- Signs of dehydration → Medical care
- Blood in stool → Evaluation
- Symptoms >3 days → Stool test
```

## 🦠 BACTERIAL INFECTIONS

### 1. **Strep Throat (Bacterial Pharyngitis)**
**Treatment:**
```
MEDICINES:
FIRST CHOICE:
- Amoxicillin 500mg - Three times daily for 10 days

PENICILLIN ALLERGY:
- Azithromycin (Zithromax) 500mg - Day 1, then 250mg for 4 days
  OR
- Cephalexin 500mg - Four times daily for 10 days

SYMPTOMATIC:
- Paracetamol for pain/fever
- Throat lozenges

VACCINES:
- None available

FOLLOW-UP:
- Complete full antibiotic course (prevent rheumatic fever)
- If no improvement in 2-3 days → Re-evaluate
```

---

### 2. **Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)**
**Treatment:**
```
MEDICINES:
UNCOMPLICATED UTI:
- Nitrofurantoin 100mg - Twice daily for 5-7 days
  OR
- Cefixime 200mg - Twice daily for 5-7 days
  OR
- Trimethoprim-Sulfamethoxazole DS - Twice daily for 3 days

COMPLICATED/RECURRENT:
- Ciprofloxacin 500mg - Twice daily for 7 days

SYMPTOMATIC:
- Phenazopyridine (Pyridium) 200mg - For pain (2-3 days max)
- Drink 8-10 glasses of water daily

VACCINES:
- None

FOLLOW-UP:
- Fever, back pain → Kidney infection (Pyelonephritis) → Urgent care
- Recurrent UTIs → Urology referral
```

---

### 3. **Pneumonia (Bacterial)**
**Treatment:**
```
MEDICINES:
OUTPATIENT (Mild-Moderate):
- Azithromycin 500mg - Day 1, then 250mg for 4 days
  OR
- Amoxicillin-Clavulanate (Augmentin) 875mg - Twice daily for 7-10 days

SEVERE (Hospitalization may be needed):
- Ceftriaxone 1-2g IV + Azithromycin

VACCINES:
- Pneumococcal Vaccine (Pneumovax 23) - Adults >65 years
- PCV13 (Prevnar 13) - Children and high-risk adults
- Annual Influenza Vaccine

FOLLOW-UP:
- Difficulty breathing → Emergency
- Chest X-ray recommended
- Follow-up X-ray in 6 weeks
```

## 💉 COMPREHENSIVE VACCINE SCHEDULE

### **Children (0-18 years)**
- Hepatitis B: Birth, 1-2 months, 6-18 months
- DTaP (Diphtheria, Tetanus, Pertussis): 2, 4, 6, 15-18 months, 4-6 years
- Polio (IPV): 2, 4, 6-18 months, 4-6 years
- MMR (Measles, Mumps, Rubella): 12-15 months, 4-6 years
- Varicella (Chickenpox): 12-15 months, 4-6 years
- Hepatitis A: 12-23 months (2 doses)
- Meningococcal: 11-12 years, 16 years

### **Adults (19-64 years)**
- Tdap (Tetanus, Diphtheria, Pertussis): Once, then Td booster every 10 years
- Influenza: Annually
- COVID-19: Primary series + boosters
- HPV: Up to age 26 (3 doses)
- Hepatitis A & B: If at risk
- Meningococcal: If at risk

### **Seniors (65+ years)**
- Pneumococcal (PCV13, PPSV23): One-time or booster
- Shingles (Herpes Zoster): 2 doses (Shingrix)
- Influenza: Annually (high-dose preferred)
- COVID-19: Keep up-to-date with boosters
- Tdap/Td: Every 10 years

## 🚨 RED FLAGS - URGENT MEDICAL ATTENTION

**Seek Emergency Care If:**
- High fever (>103°F/39.4°C) not responding to medication
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Chest pain
- Severe headache with stiff neck
- Confusion or altered mental status
- Seizures
- Signs of dehydration (no urination, extreme thirst, dizziness)
- Severe abdominal pain
- Bleeding or bruising easily
- Rash with fever (could be meningitis)

---

## ⚠️ IMPORTANT PRESCRIBING NOTES

1. **Always check for allergies** before prescribing
2. **Pregnant/Breastfeeding:** Many medications contraindicated
3. **Children:** Dose by weight (mg/kg)
4. **Elderly:** Start with lower doses, adjust for kidney/liver function
5. **Drug Interactions:** Check current medications
6. **Complete Course:** Especially antibiotics (prevent resistance)
7. **Follow-up:** Schedule based on condition severity
